  - question: "Are PET cold cups microwave safe?"
    answer: "No. PET cups soften around 160°F and will deform in a microwave. Use them for cold and iced beverages only — iced coffee, smoothies, juice, soft drinks, cold tea. For hot beverages, use paper hot cups."
  - question: "What size PET cold cups are most common in foodservice?"
    answer: "12oz, 16oz, 20oz, 24oz, and 32oz are the standard sizes. 16oz and 24oz are the highest-volume in fast-casual restaurants and coffee shops. Bubble tea operators typically run 16oz and 24oz with a 95mm or 98mm rim for sealable lids."
  - question: "Do PET cold cups stack?"
    answer: "Yes. Most PET cup designs include a stacking rib near the rim that prevents jamming during dispensing. Case packs are typically 500 or 1000 units, palletized for efficient warehouse handling."
  - question: "What rim diameter should I order?"
    answer: "Match the rim to your lid stock. 78mm is the most common small rim (12oz cups), 92mm and 95mm are mid-range (16oz and 20oz cups), 98mm fits larger sizes. Mismatched rims will not seal properly — order cups and lids as a paired system."
  - question: "Are PET cups eco-friendly?"
    answer: "PET is recyclable (resin code #1), the most widely-accepted rigid plastic in US curbside programs. It is not compostable. For compostable cold cup applications, use PLA cups — but note that PLA requires industrial composting facilities, not home compost bins."
  - question: "Can PET cold cups hold hot liquids?"
    answer: "No. Even briefly. PET will warp, leach plasticizers, and potentially fail under hot liquid load. Always pair the cup material to the beverage temperature."

**PET cold cups** are clear plastic cups used across foodservice for cold beverages — iced coffee, sodas, juices, smoothies, bubble tea, and cold-brew tea. PET (polyethylene terephthalate, recycling code #1) has the highest optical clarity of any food-safe plastic, which sells iced beverages on visual appeal alone. Unlike polystyrene, PET is impact-resistant and won't crack at fridge or freezer temperatures.

## When to choose PET cold cups

| Beverage type | Right cup | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Iced coffee, soda, juice | **PET cold cup** | Clarity, freezer-safe, durable |
| Hot coffee, tea, soup | **Paper hot cup** | Heat-stable, insulated |
| Bubble tea, smoothies | **PET cold cup** (95–98mm rim) | Fits sealing machines |
| Compostable cold cup (eco-mandate markets) | **PLA cold cup** | BPI compostable |
| Foam cup (legacy, now banned 10+ states) | **PET or paper cold cup** | EPS bans |

PET has effectively replaced polystyrene (PS) cold cups across the industry. PS is more brittle, more prone to splitting, and styrene's reputation has eroded acceptance among end-customers.
