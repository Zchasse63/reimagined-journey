  - question: "What does \"SOS\" mean for paper bags?"
    answer: "SOS = Self-Opening Sack. The bag has a square bottom that pops open into shape on its own when filled, without manual squaring. SOS is the dominant takeout-bag format for restaurants and delicatessens because it stands upright on the counter while orders are packed."
  - question: "How do I pick the right SOS bag size?"
    answer: "SOS bags are sized by capacity (#4, #6, #8, #12, #20). #4 is small (single sandwich), #6 is the lunch standard, #12 is a hearty single-meal takeout, #20 is family-meal or grocery-style. Match bag size to your average ticket size — oversize bags waste material and look sloppy; undersize bags require double-bagging."
  - question: "Are SOS bags kraft or white?"
    answer: "Both. Natural kraft (brown) is the traditional and lowest-cost option, also the preferred eco-positioned look. Bleached white is used by brands targeting a cleaner, more upscale aesthetic. Recycled-content kraft is increasingly common."
  - question: "Can SOS bags be custom printed?"
    answer: "Yes. Logo print on natural kraft is the most common — 1-color or 2-color flexo print runs at ~50,000 unit minimums with 6–8 week lead times. White bags accept more vivid printing but cost ~20% more per bag."
  - question: "Are SOS bags recyclable or compostable?"
    answer: "Both. Unbleached kraft paper is recyclable in any paper stream and compostable in any program that accepts uncoated paper. Watch for grease saturation — heavily oily food can contaminate the paper for recycling, but compost-grade saturation is fine."
  - question: "What's the difference between SOS and twisted-handle bags?"
    answer: "SOS bags have no handles — they're carried by holding the top edge. Twisted-handle bags have rope-style handles attached for customer carrying comfort. Use SOS for in-store pickup (counter to door); use twisted-handle for delivery, catering, or boutique retail where the customer carries the bag further."

**SOS bags** (Self-Opening Sack) are the standard takeout paper bag — square bottom, no handles, opens flat on the counter for fast packing. Made from natural kraft or bleached white paper, sized by capacity from #4 (small) up to #20 (grocery-format). The right bag for restaurant counter takeout, delicatessen orders, and any application where the customer carries the bag a short distance.

## Size selection by ticket type

| Bag size | Typical capacity | Use case |
| --- | --- | --- |
| #4 | 1 sandwich | Bakery items, single sandwich |
| #6 | Lunch combo | Most common quick-service takeout |
| #8 | Multi-item lunch | Sandwich + side + drink (small) |
| #12 | Hearty dinner | Family-meal takeout, multiple entrées |
| #20 | Bulk / grocery format | Catering pickup, retail produce |

## Specifications at a glance

| Property | Kraft SOS paper bag |
| --- | --- |
| Material | Natural kraft or bleached white paper |
| Basis weight | 30# to 65# (heavier = stronger, more expensive) |
| Recyclable | Yes (paper stream) |
| Compostable | Yes (in any program accepting paper) |
| Heat-resistant for grease/hot food | Yes (kraft handles temperature well; grease shows through) |
| Custom-print MOQ | ~50,000 units typical |
| Standard case packs | 250–500 depending on bag size |
