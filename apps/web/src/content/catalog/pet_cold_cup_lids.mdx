faqs:
  - question: "How do I match a lid to my cold cup?"
    answer: "Match the rim diameter in millimeters. 78mm rim cups take 78mm lids; 92mm cups take 92mm lids; 95mm and 98mm follow the same pattern. The lid manufacturer's rim spec must match the cup manufacturer's rim spec — even within a single rim size, lids from different vendors don't always interchange because the snap profile varies."
  - question: "What's the difference between a flat lid, a dome lid, and a sip lid?"
    answer: "A flat lid is the lowest profile and best for stacking; it usually has a slot for a straw. A dome lid has a raised top, used for whipped toppings, foam, or visual presentation on smoothies and bubble tea. A sip lid (drink-through lid) has a built-in straw-replacement aperture — common in straw-ban markets."
  - question: "Why do some lids have an X-slot vs a straight slot?"
    answer: "An X-slot grips the straw better — it holds the straw in place during transit so it doesn't slide out or shift during delivery. Straight slots are slightly faster to insert and are still common in dine-in operations where transit is short."
  - question: "Are PET lids the right choice for hot drinks?"
    answer: "No. PET lids are for cold cups only. For hot beverages, use PE-coated paper hot cup lids (white or black PS dome lids are also common but increasingly being phased out by foam bans). Pair lid material to beverage temperature."
  - question: "Can I use PET lids on paper cups?"
    answer: "Sometimes — but only if the paper cup's rim spec matches the lid's rim spec exactly. Most paper cold cups are paired with paper-specific lids that have a slightly different snap geometry. Order the lid that pairs with your specific cup SKU rather than mixing brands."
  - question: "How are PET lids packaged for shipping?"
    answer: "Typically 1000 lids per case. Stacked, sleeved, then boxed. Cube efficiency is higher than for cups themselves (~1 lid case per 2–4 cup cases of the same rim size)."

**PET cold cup lids** are the snap-on covers that pair with PET cold cups for iced beverages. The lid material matches the cup material — PET to PET — so the cup-and-lid system shares the same recycling stream and the same temperature stability. Most lids come in three profile variants: flat (with straw slot), dome (for whipped/foamed beverages), and sip-through (for straw-ban markets).

## Lid selection cheat sheet

| If you sell... | Use this lid type |
| --- | --- |
| Iced coffee, soda, juice with a straw | Flat lid with X-slot |
| Smoothies, milkshakes, beverages with whipped topping | Dome lid (4-Section or wide) |
| Bubble tea | Flat lid (often sealed with a sealing machine instead) |
| Markets with straw bans (CA, NY, etc.) | Sip-through lid (no straw needed) |
| Cold-only takeout in winter / outdoors | Flat lid with X-slot |

## Specifications at a glance

| Property | PET cold cup lid |
| --- | --- |
| Material | PET (matches cup, recycling code #1) |
| Temperature range | -40°F to ~150°F |
| Microwave safe | No |
| Recyclable | Yes (#1, where accepted) |
| Common rim sizes | 78mm, 92mm, 95mm, 98mm |
| Typical case pack | 1000 |
| Profile variants | Flat (X-slot or straight), Dome, Sip-through |

**Order cups and lids as a paired system.** Lids from different vendors don't always interchange even within the same rim size — snap geometry can vary by 0.5–1mm and result in either a too-loose or too-tight fit. When you migrate cup vendors, plan to migrate the lid vendor at the same time.
