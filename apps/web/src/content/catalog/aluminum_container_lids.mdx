faqs:
  - question: "What's the difference between a board lid, an aluminum lid, and a dome lid?"
    answer: "A board lid is foil-laminated paperboard — lightweight, low cost, and the standard pairing for transport. An aluminum lid is solid aluminum, used for steam-table covers and oven service where heat resistance matters. A plastic dome lid is clear, used when you want product visibility (for cold display or self-service catering setups)."
  - question: "Do lids match across vendors?"
    answer: "Within standard sizes (full, half, quarter, round 7\", round 9\"), most US-spec aluminum pans use the same overall pan dimensions and lids fit interchangeably. That said, \"snap\" geometry varies and a tight fit isn't guaranteed across brands. Buy pans and lids as a paired system when you can."
  - question: "Are board lids oven safe?"
    answer: "Board lids (foil-laminated paperboard) are designed for transport and steam-table holding, NOT for oven use. The paperboard scorches at ~350°F. For oven-cooked applications, use solid aluminum lids or remove the lid during baking and replace it after."
  - question: "Can I microwave food in an aluminum pan with a lid on?"
    answer: "No. Aluminum pans (and aluminum lids) cannot go in a microwave under any circumstances. Transfer food to a microwavable container (PP or MFPP) before reheating, or use a plastic dome lid + microwave (some plastic dome lids are MFPP-rated)."
  - question: "What gauge are board lids?"
    answer: "Board lids are spec'd by weight in grams (full-size lids run ~49g, half ~19g, etc.) and by the foil lamination thickness. Heavier board lids resist warping during steam-table holding longer."
  - question: "How are lids packaged?"
    answer: "Typically 50 lids per case for full-size, 100 for half-size, 200–500 for smaller sizes. Pallet density varies. Lids ship more cube-efficiently than pans, so combined orders pack denser when you buy matching pan/lid sets."

**Aluminum container lids** pair with aluminum foodservice pans for transport, holding, and oven service. Three lid types cover virtually all catering and takeout scenarios: foil-laminated board lids (transport), solid aluminum lids (oven and steam table), and plastic dome lids (display). Lid choice depends on whether you need heat resistance, visibility, or low cost.

## Lid type selection cheat sheet

| Scenario | Right lid | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Catering transport, no reheating | Board lid (foil-laminated paperboard) | Lightweight, low cost |
| Steam-table holding | Aluminum lid | Heat-stable at holding temps |
| Oven baking with cover | Aluminum lid (solid foil) | Oven safe; board lid will scorch |
| Cold/refrigerated display | Plastic dome lid (PET or PP) | Visibility |
| Family takeout meal | Board lid or PP dome lid | Cost vs visibility tradeoff |

## Specifications at a glance

| Lid type | Material | Heat | Recyclable |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Board lid | Foil-laminated paperboard | Up to ~200°F | No (mixed material) |
| Aluminum lid | Solid aluminum foil | Up to ~500°F (oven) | Yes (when clean) |
| Plastic dome lid (PET) | PET (#1) | Up to ~150°F | Yes (#1, where accepted) |
| Plastic dome lid (PP) | Polypropylene (#5) | Up to ~230°F | Rarely accepted (#5) |

**Always order pan and lid as a matched pair.** Mismatched sizes are the single most common back-of-house catering error and result in tape-and-foil patches that don't hold during transport.
