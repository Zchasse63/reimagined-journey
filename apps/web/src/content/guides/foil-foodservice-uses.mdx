Aluminum foil is the most underrated workhorse in a restaurant kitchen. It wraps individual items, covers catering pans, lines sheet pans for prep, and seals leftovers for refrigeration. Picking the right gauge and format affects labor, food quality, and total foil spend — which has been reshaped by Section 301 tariffs over the past four years. This guide covers gauge selection, format choice, and the current sourcing landscape.

## Foil formats — roll vs pop-up sheets

<CatalogLink slug="foil_rolls_sheets" />

Aluminum foil comes in two foodservice formats:

**Bulk rolls** — the traditional format. 500 or 1000 feet per roll, dispensed from a wall-mount or counter rack. Lowest cost per square inch.

**Pop-up sheets** — interfolded, pre-cut sheets dispensed one at a time from a box (like tissues). Higher cost per sheet but faster at the wrap station.

**When to choose pop-up sheets:**
- High-volume wrap stations (burrito assembly, sandwich wrap)
- Multiple stations operating simultaneously (each needs its own dispenser)
- Operations where wrap labor cost > foil material cost
- The break-even is typically 100+ wraps per shift per station

**When to choose bulk rolls:**
- Pan covers, full-meal entrée wraps (larger surface area)
- Lower-volume kitchens or catering prep
- Lining sheet pans (need custom-sized pieces)
- Premium operations with custom cut sizes

## Gauge selection — match thickness to application

| Gauge | Thickness | Application | Cost vs standard |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Standard** | ~0.6 mil | Room-temp wraps, refrigerator storage, single-meal wraps | Baseline |
| **Heavy-duty** | ~0.9–1.0 mil | Oven baking, grilling, hot-food wraps | +30–50% |
| **Extra-heavy** | ~1.3–1.5 mil | Restaurant oven volume, dense food, premium catering | +60–100% |

**Standard foil** is the workhorse. Most takeout wraps don't need more — the food is warm but not requiring oven exposure. Standard foil tears more easily than heavy-duty but the cost difference makes it the right default for high-volume wrap stations.

**Heavy-duty foil** is required for any oven application. The standard gauge can tear or burn under sustained oven heat or under the weight of a heavy entrée being wrapped tight. Use heavy-duty for grilling, baking, and any application where the wrapped item goes back through heat.

**Extra-heavy foil** is for restaurant-grade oven service — the kind of repeated high-heat use where standard foil would degrade. Also for premium catering where the foil maintains structural shape under heavy food weight. The cost premium is substantial; reserve for applications that justify it.

## Pop-up dispensers vs box-and-counter format

The pop-up sheet category itself has variants:

**Pre-loaded dispensers** — a wall-mount or counter unit that holds the foil box and dispenses one sheet per pull. Standard for high-volume wrap stations.

**Box-and-counter format** — a freestanding box that sits next to the wrap station. Operator pulls sheets one at a time. Lower cost than dispensers but less ergonomic at speed.

Pop-up sheet sizing:
- **9 × 10.75 inches** — single burrito, sandwich, taco wrap
- **12 × 10.75 inches** — large sandwich, hoagie, full-meal wrap

Most operations standardize on one size to simplify station setup. 12 × 10.75 is the more common single-size choice because it handles smaller items adequately by folding.

## Section 301 tariff impact — the 2026 sourcing reality

Aluminum foil is one of the most tariff-affected categories in foodservice packaging. China-origin aluminum foil carries:

- **Base HTS duty (7607.11.60):** 5.4%
- **Section 301 (China-only):** 25% additional
- **Antidumping duty (AD):** 48.64–106.09%
- **Countervailing duty (CVD):** 17.14–80.97%

Combined effective rate: **130%+** since 2024.

The result: US distributors have spent 2022–2026 actively shifting sourcing away from China. Major source countries now include:
- **Turkey** — ~25% of US aluminum foil imports
- **Korea** — ~15%
- **Vietnam** — ~10%
- **Domestic US** — ~30% (up from under 5% pre-2018)

For the full picture, see our [Section 301 tariff guide](/guides/section-301-tariff-foodservice-packaging/).

**Buyer implication:** When evaluating foil pricing across distributors in 2026, ask about source country. A "competitive" price on China-origin foil includes the tariff layered into landed cost — and may be vulnerable to AD/CVD review adjustments. Multi-source distributors offer more pricing stability.

## Foil + pan pairings

Aluminum pans (steam-table pans, round catering pans) are the natural complement to foil. Common pairings:

<CatalogLink slug="aluminum_containers" cta="Browse aluminum pans →" />

| Application | Pan | Lid / cover |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Catering transport | 13g pan | Foil-laminated board lid OR heavy-duty foil sheet |
| Oven baking + steam table | 13g pan | Solid aluminum lid OR heavy-duty foil |
| Reheating prepared meal | 13g pan | Foil + plastic dome lid combo |
| Premium catering display | 33g pan | Solid aluminum lid |
| Family takeout meal | 13g round | Plastic dome lid (visibility) |

Use foil sheets as a cover when ordering pans without dedicated lids — heavy-duty foil cut to size handles the same transport role as a board lid at lower per-unit cost. This works for catering operations doing same-day transport but is less ideal for steam-table holding (the foil doesn't fully seal during multi-hour service).

## Specialty foil products

Beyond standard rolls and pop-up sheets, foodservice operations sometimes use:

**Pre-cut interfolded sheets** — non-pop-up sheets in a stack, used for menu items requiring a specific consistent size

**Non-stick aluminum foil** — silicone-coated on one side, for grilling fish and other items that stick to plain foil. Higher cost per piece, niche application

**Heavy-duty interfolded** — for grilling stations where each item gets a heavy-duty piece

For most operations, plain standard + heavy-duty in roll format handles 95% of need. Specialty SKUs only earn their place at scale.
