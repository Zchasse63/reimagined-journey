Bubble tea cup operations are different from standard cold-beverage operations because of the heat-sealing workflow. The cup, the film, and the sealing machine must all be compatible — and small mismatches cause leak issues, jam sealers, or produce ragged seal edges. This guide covers the integrated system.

For PET cup rim sizes generally see [PET Cup Rim Sizes](/guides/pet-cup-rim-sizes-78-92-95-98mm/). For the broader cup overview see [Hot vs Cold Cup Selection](/guides/cup-selection-hot-cold/).

## The system: cup + film + sealer

A complete bubble tea station has three integrated components:

1. **Cup** — PET cold cup, typically 16-24oz at 95mm or 98mm rim
2. **Film** — PE or PP film roll, sized to fit the cup rim
3. **Sealer** — heat-sealing machine that presses film onto the cup rim

The sealer applies heat (~250-300°F) for 1-2 seconds, melting the film onto the rim. When done correctly, the seal is leakproof under shaking and only tears with intentional straw insertion.

Each component constrains the others. A 95mm sealer requires 95mm cups and 95mm-sized film cutting. Don't try to mix sizes within a single station.

## Cup specifications

Bubble tea cups have specific requirements beyond standard PET cold cups:

**Rim diameter:** 95mm or 98mm (must match sealer)

**Cup material:** PET cold cup standard (#1 plastic)

**Rim profile:** Must be flat with no raised edge that interferes with film contact. Standard PET cold cups (with flat rims) work; cups designed for snap-lid sealing with raised rim edges don't.

**Size range:** 16oz, 20oz, 24oz are most common. 12oz and 32oz are available but less standard.

**Pattern / printing:** Cups themselves are typically clear (showing the beverage). Some operations use printed cups (logo or pattern on the cup wall) — the seal film provides the top-surface branding opportunity.

## Film specifications

**Material:** PE (polyethylene) is dominant for the customer-experience reasons — easier tear with straw, softer feel. PP (polypropylene) is used in higher-end operations or for shipping/delivery where durability matters.

**Thickness:** 50-80 microns typical. Thicker film is more durable; thinner film tears more easily for the customer. Most operations land at 60-65 microns.

**Roll size:** Standard roll is 100-200 meters (enough for 3,000-6,000 seals depending on cup size). High-volume operations use 500-meter rolls.

**Print options:**
- **Stock (unprinted)** — clear or pre-printed generic patterns ("Bubble Tea," logo of major franchises, drink-specific designs)
- **Custom printed** — your logo or design, 50,000-100,000 unit MOQ, 6-8 week lead time
- **Hot-foil stamping** — premium metallic effects on the seal (additional cost)

For new operations, start with stock unprinted or generic stock-printed film. Move to custom-printed once you've stabilized operations and want the brand reinforcement.

## Sealer comparison

| Sealer type | Capacity | Cost range | Use case |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Manual benchtop | 5-10 seals/min | $400-800 | Single-location, low volume (under 200 drinks/day) |
| Semi-auto floor | 10-20 seals/min | $1,500-3,500 | Standard fast-casual bubble tea (200-500/day) |
| Fully automated | 20-40+ seals/min | $5,000-15,000+ | High-volume, multi-station operations |

**Manual sealer:** The operator places each cup, lowers the sealer, waits for the seal, removes the cup. Slowest but lowest cost. For under-200-drink-per-day operations, manual is workable.

**Semi-auto:** The operator places the cup in a holder, hits a button, the machine cycles automatically. The standard for most bubble tea shops. Cycle time 2-3 seconds per cup.

**Fully automated:** Multi-cup holders, automatic feed of film, programmable settings. Used by chain bubble tea operations with high single-store volume.

When choosing, account not just for unit cost but for staff training, maintenance, and parts availability. Domestic sealers (US-manufactured) have better parts availability than imported sealers from China or Taiwan, especially for replacement heating elements.

## Operational workflow

A typical bubble tea seal operation:

1. **Prepare drink** — assemble beverage in 95mm or 98mm PET cup
2. **Wipe rim** — clean any drink residue from cup rim (rim debris ruins seal quality)
3. **Position cup in sealer holder** — cup must seat correctly to seal evenly
4. **Apply seal** — press button or close manual lever
5. **Wait 1-2 seconds** — for seal application
6. **Remove cup** — film is now adhered to rim
7. **Hand to customer** — they break the film with their straw

Total time per cup: 3-5 seconds after drink assembly. A trained operator can run 12-20 drinks per minute through a semi-auto sealer.

## Common operational failures

**Seal not adhering at all** — Sealer temperature is too low, or film is wrong material for the temperature setting. Increase temp or switch to lower-melt film.

**Seal partially adhering (creases visible)** — Cup rim wasn't clean. Wipe rim before sealing.

**Seal too tight to tear with straw** — Sealer temperature is too high, fusing the film too aggressively. Decrease temp.

**Film tears during sealing** — Film thickness is below the sealer's spec. Use thicker film.

**Cup jams in sealer holder** — Cup rim size doesn't match the holder. Verify 95mm vs 98mm.

**Sealer producing inconsistent results** — Heating element is failing (common after 1-2 years of heavy use). Replace heating element.

## Cost reality

Approximate operational cost per sealed bubble tea drink:

| Component | Per-drink cost |
| --- | --- |
| 24oz PET cup, 95mm | $0.08-0.11 |
| PE film seal | $0.005-0.012 |
| Sealer amortized (2-year lifetime, $2,500 cost, 200/day × 600 days) | $0.021/drink |
| **Total cup+film+sealer cost per drink** | **$0.11-0.14** |

For a 300-drink-per-day bubble tea operation, that's about $35-42/day, $9,000-11,000/year. Most of the cost is the cup itself, not the sealing.

## Setup checklist for new bubble tea operations

1. **Choose cup rim size first** — 95mm is more universal; 98mm gives you larger cup options
2. **Source sealer matching that rim size** — confirm with vendor before purchase
3. **Order film in the right size and material** — PE for customer experience, 60-65 micron thickness
4. **Buy cups, film, and sealer as a package from one vendor initially** — ensures compatibility
5. **Train staff on rim-wiping discipline** — biggest single quality variable
6. **Plan for sealer replacement parts** — order spare heating elements upfront
7. **Test custom-print film at low MOQ before committing to full run** — verify design before printing 50,000+ rolls

| Your operation | Spec |
